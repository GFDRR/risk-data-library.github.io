The project

The goal of the project is to make risk data significantly easier to work with so that risk assessments can be performed faster, more affordably and more frequently. To do that, we are developing a series of tools and resources designed explicitly for disaster risk assessment and the people who perform them. The first two components have been developed: the risk-data schema and accompanying database.

The schema

The risk data schema is simply a language for describing risk data—hazard, exposure, vulnerability and modelled loss—in a consistent, accessible manner. Datasets formatted using the schema are both machine and human readable, and highly interoperable. The schema covers critical metadata that risk experts need to identify datasets contents, storing this within the datasets themselves where it cannot become separated.

The value of the schema grows with each dataset it is applied to. Widespread use is critical. The broad plan is to build support for the schema within the risk community; then use that support to encourage the official adoption of the schema by one or more leading funders of new risk data such as The World Bank. This will result in a rapid expansion of the number of schema-formatted datasets available to the risk community.

The database

The risk database has been developed to meet the need to better share data developed in risk assessments. The database was developed to address the challenges of storing and sharing risk data. Its architecture stores hazard, exposure, vulnerability and modeled loss datasets in the same environment, keeping data from the same analysis connected. Teams and organisations will no longer need to store different types of risk data on separate databases, making it easier to locate for use in assessments.

API access is supported, making it easier to share risk datasets and laying the foundation for a network of databases that are able to be accessed via a single search.

We have invited a small group of data and risk specialists such as yourself to look over these components prior to public release. There are three things we are hoping to achieve with this:

Feedback requested

We are looking for your insights and suggestions on the following points:

The database

  • How well the current structure is likely to meet the data storage needs of projects and organisations in the risk community, particularly those related to performing risk assessments.
  • Any reasons the current database design might become unstable or be otherwise ineffective over time or when dealing with large numbers of datasets and/or users.
  • Ways the current structure could be improved to make the database more efficient, or how it might be adapted to other roles within the risk community.
  • Which data-export formats would best meet the needs of the risk community.

The schema

  • How well the current schema describes the four types of risk data.
  • How well the current schema supports the information needs of risk assessments. Particularly whether it describes an appropriate range of metadata to enable risk experts to identify the datasets they need for a particular disaster risk assessment.
  • Any critical information that is not currently described in the schema, or any surplus information that could be removed without compromising the utility of the schema.
  • How understandable the current schema is and anything that might make it easier for risk experts to understand it without having to rely on external legends or guides.
